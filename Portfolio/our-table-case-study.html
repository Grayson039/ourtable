The Origin

It started at our table

Like a lot of people, my household decided it was time to make a real change in our eating habits. We wanted to cook more, eat better, and be more intentional about what went on the table each week. The problem? We couldn't find a single app that actually fit how we live.

Some apps were great at saving recipes from TikTok but hopeless at building a grocery list. Others had decent meal planners but didn't account for what we already had in the fridge. None of them understood that "we" meant multiple people with different dietary preferences all sitting down at the same table.

"We needed to make a change in our eating habits and couldn't find an app that fit our needs. That gap was the product."

That gap — between the recipes you save and the meals you actually cook — became the product. Our Table is built around the idea that a recipe app should work for your entire household, not just you.

Key Insights

Three things every competitor gets wrong

The research surfaced three structural gaps — not just missing features, but fundamentally wrong assumptions about who is using these apps and how.

👨‍👩‍👧

Nobody builds for the household

Every competing app is designed for a single user. Real kitchens have multiple people with different dietary needs, preferences, and restrictions. This assumption is invisible but it breaks everything.

💳

Paywalls are destroying trust

The single biggest complaint across all four apps, across thousands of reviews, is hitting a paywall mid-use. Users feel tricked. This creates a massive opening for any app that is simply honest about its pricing.

❄️

The fridge is the missing piece

Every app starts at "save a recipe." None of them start with "what do you already have?" The bridge between what's in your fridge and what's on your plate is unbuilt — and it's the most valuable feature to build.

The Solution

Our Table — designed for real households

Our Table is built around three pillars: Save anything. Know your fridge. Cook for everyone. Each feature is designed to close a gap that competitors leave open — not just as a feature add, but as a fundamentally different approach to what a recipe app should be.

Voice navigation, auto step timers, screen always on. Full-screen readable text. No competitor has voice control.

Export any recipe or collection as a clean, ad-free PDF. AirPrint support. Deglaze users literally can't print at all.

📖

Family Recipe Book UNIQUE

Scan handwritten recipe cards — original image preserved alongside parsed text. Add stories and memories. Export as a cookbook PDF gift.

🎉

Party & Event Mode UNIQUE

Scale recipes for a number of guests, handle guest dietary restrictions, generate one combined shopping list for the event.

🖥️

Web & Desktop Access FIXES GAP

Full browser experience for managing recipes from a laptop. Planned for v2 — directly addresses Deglaze's #1 complaint.

⚠️

Every ingredient and recipe is checked against your household's dietary restrictions in real time. Conflicting items are flagged inline — showing which household member is affected — with an instant AI-suggested substitute. Accept the swap and it updates your recipe automatically.

Product Process

How this was built

This case study represents a full product discovery and definition process — from personal frustration to investor-ready documentation — executed before a single line of app code was written.

1

Problem Definition

Articulated the personal and household pain point. Validated that the frustration was not unique to us — it was a market-wide gap.

2

Competitor Research & Review Analysis

Systematically studied four leading apps — ReciMe, Flavorish, Deglaze, and Recipe Keeper — across App Store reviews, third-party ratings, Reddit discussions, and feature comparisons. Identified recurring pain themes: aggressive paywalls, no household awareness, no desktop access, no printing.

3

Feature Ideation & Prioritization

Generated 12+ differentiated feature concepts mapped to specific competitor gaps. Categorized as "nobody does this," "we do it better," and "fixes a known complaint." Prioritized using a v1/v2/v3 release framework.

4

Open Source Stack Research

Identified relevant GitHub repositories to build on: Mealie (backend architecture reference), recipe-scrapers (blog/URL parsing), Open Food Facts iOS (barcode lookup), and food recognition models for fridge scanning — dramatically reducing build time and cost.

5

Full Product Requirements Document

Produced a comprehensive PRD covering user personas, detailed feature specifications, technical architecture, competitive positioning, pricing strategy, success metrics, and a phased roadmap. Ready for developer handoff.

6

UI Design & Visual Identity

Color system finalized: terracotta, off-white cream, and olive green for light mode; deep navy and burnt orange for dark mode. All 8 core screens built as fully interactive HTML prototypes — Auth & Onboarding, Home, Recipe Detail, Add Recipe, Fridge & Pantry, Grocery List, Profile & Settings, and Recipe Book. SwiftUI prototype and App Store strategy next.
